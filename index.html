Transcript Part 1 · Screen 18 of 30

Key Lines from the Trailer

Tap the blue words to see grammar explanations.

⏱ 0:48 KRATOS: "Do not be sorry. Be better."
Do not + base verb = Negative imperative.
This is how we give a direct command NOT to do something.
Informal: Don't be sorry.   Formal: Do not be sorry.
Kratos uses the formal version — it sounds more serious, authoritative.
Be + adjective = Affirmative imperative with the verb 'to be'.
Hidden subject: You be better → Be better.
In imperatives, the subject 'you' is always implied but never said.
This line is one of the most famous in God of War.
⏱ 1:05 KRATOS: "Close your heart to it. Show no mercy."
Close (your heart) = Affirmative imperative — base verb directly, no subject.
You close your heart → Close your heart.
Kratos is ordering Atreus to detach emotionally — a very harsh command.
Show (no mercy) = Another imperative. Notice how ALL of Kratos's commands are short, direct, and brutal. No "please" — only orders. This reveals his character.
⏱ 2:10 KRATOS: "Remember this feeling. Do not let it go."
Remember = A softer imperative — Kratos is asking Atreus to feel and internalise a moment. Not a command to fight, but to remember. A rare emotional depth from him.
Do not + base verb = Negative imperative again. Full form sounds more formal and powerful than "don't". Perfect for Kratos's character — always heavy, always commanding.
Transcript Part 2 · Screen 19 of 30

More Key Lines

⏱ 2:28 KRATOS: "The cycle ends here. We must be better than this."
be better here is NOT a simple imperative — it follows "must", making it a modal construction: must be.
Compare: "Be better!" (imperative — direct command) vs "We must be better." (obligation/necessity). Small word change = big meaning shift!
⏱ 0:22 — Kratos training Atreus KRATOS: "Focus. Breathe. Draw."
Focus. — One-word imperative. In English, a single verb can be a complete command. Very common in action/military contexts: Focus. Stop. Listen. Run.
Breathe. — Another one-word imperative. Notice Kratos gives instructions in short bursts — this creates urgency and tension in the scene.
Draw. — Imperative meaning "draw your bow" / "pull back the arrow." In context, this is a hunting instruction. Short. Powerful. No wasted words.

Grammar Rule · Screen 21 of 30

The Imperative — Direct Commands

TypeStructureGod of War Example
✅ AffirmativeBase verb + restBe better. Close your heart. Show mercy.
❌ NegativeDo not / Don't + base verbDo not be sorry. Don't give up.
⚔ With "be"Be + adjective/nounBe ready. Be worthy. Be better.
⚡ One wordSingle verbFocus. Breathe. Draw.

When do we use imperatives?

Commands

"Stop. Look at me."

Advice

"Be patient. Trust the process."

Warnings

"Do not enter this realm."

Instructions

"Turn left. Follow the path."

A Real Conversation

Two students are talking about the trailer. Read the dialogue — notice how the vocabulary and grammar are used naturally.

🧑
Did you watch the God of War trailer? What did you think?
👩
Yes! It was amazing. I was surprised by how emotional it felt. I expected more action, but the relationship between the father and son really hit me.

👩

🧑
What about the line "The cycle ends here"? What do you think the cycle is?
👩
I think it's the pattern of violence in his past. He was the god of war — he destroyed everything. Now he wants to survive and live differently, for his son's sake.
🧑
That's really deep for a video game! Do you think he's a worthy father?
👩
In some ways yes — he protects Atreus with everything he has. But he doesn't show mercy or kindness. That's the tragic part. He doesn't know how to love without being cold.

Decision Scenario · Screen 27 of 30

You Are Atreus

You are Atreus. You just missed an important shot during the hunt. The animal escaped. Kratos looks at you silently. Then he says: "Again. This time, do not hesitate."

How do you respond?

🏹
Try again immediately
You say nothing. You pick up your bow and prepare. Actions speak louder than words.
🙏
Apologise first
"I'm sorry, father. I wasn't focused." You need to acknowledge the mistake before moving on.
❓
Ask for guidance
"What am I doing wrong? Help me understand." You want to learn, not just try again blindly.
💭
Push back
"I hesitated because I wasn't sure. Tell me why it matters." You need a reason to fight.
Defend Your Decision
